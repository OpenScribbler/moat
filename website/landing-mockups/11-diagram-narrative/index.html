Step 01 · Signed

Publisher signs.

Add one GitHub Actions workflow. Moat generates a signed manifest, anchors it to a transparency log, and pushes it to your registry branch.

Step 02 · Attested

Registry attests.

The registry indexes the signed manifest. Anyone can query what was attested, when, and by whom.

Step 03 · Verified

Consumer verifies.

Run moat-verify before installing. It checks the content hash against the registry attestation and confirms the signature is logged in Rekor. Tampered content fails.

Skills run with your credentials.

AI agent content installs directly into your environment and runs with your credentials. There is currently no standard way to know whether a skill you installed came from who you think, or whether it was modified after the publisher signed off.

Moat makes tampering detectable — using your existing CI identity, not keys you have to manage.

Moat covers content provenance and integrity. It does not score safety, evaluate behavior, or enforce policy. What you choose to trust is your call. What you can verify, Moat makes verifiable.
